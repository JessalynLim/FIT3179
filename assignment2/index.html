Background Information

The +–=÷× Tour (pronounced The Mathematics Tour) is the ongoing fourth concert tour by English singer-songwriter Ed Sheeran. Comprising of 89 concerts across three legs, the tour commenced on 23 April 2022 in Dublin, Ireland, and was originally scheduled to conclude on 23 September 2023 in Inglewood, California. A rescheduled concert will be held on 28 October 2023 as a replacement for the cancelled 9 September 2023 concert in Paradise, Nevada. Thus, this concert is not included.

The tour is in support of his fifth studio album = (pronounced Equals), released 2021, and his sixth studio album − (pronounced Subtract), released 2023. Due to – being released after the Europe leg, songs from – were only occassionally performed. Songs from – were also performed on 24 June 2023, when Ed Sheeran performed as both opening and main act. Additionally, some songs from his seventh studio album Autumn Variations, released 2023, were performed at his final show in Inglewood, California.



Revenue Around the World

The Mathematic Tour is considered a world tour, however, Ed Sheeran only performed in Europe, Oceania, and North America. Not one concert was performed in Africa nor Asia.

Ed Sheeran performed multiple concerts at several locations, including major cities such as London (England), Cardiff (Wales), Frankfurt (Germany), Brisbane (Australia), Aucklund (New Zealand), and East Rutherford (United States).

Popularity

The chart above shows the popularity of tracks on Ed Sheeran' first five albums according to Spotify, with tracks that are on the Mathematics Tour setlist highlighted by a star.

The popularity of a track is a value between 0 and 100, with 100 being the most popular. The popularity is calculated by algorithm and is based, in the most part, on the total number of plays the track has had and how recent those plays are. Generally speaking, songs that are being played a lot now will have a higher popularity than songs that were played a lot in the past.

The most popular songs on the setlist were Perfect and Shape of You, both of which are also from ÷. These are followed by Photograph and Shivers, which are from × and =, respectively.


While ÷ only had four songs performed (compared to five songs from each of = and x), those four songs were some of the most popular from that album, including Castle on the Hill and Galway Girl, which both had a rating of 81.

The least popular song on the setlist was Afterglow, which has a popularity of 42. The track was released as a single as well as a special edition of =. The second least popular song on the setlist was BLOW; interestingly, this was the second song on the setlist.

Additionally, the boxplot shows that Ed Sheeran's most popular album is ÷, followed by =, and then ×. The least popular is +, but this album also appears to have the least amount of variation. × has the greatest amount of variation, ranging from 0 to 87.

Audio Features (Setlist)

The chart above shows the values of the audio features of the songs on the setlist according to Spotify.

Spotify's descriptions of these features are as following:

Acousticness - confidence measure from 0.0 to 1.0 of whether the track is acoustic; the closer the value is to 1.0, the greater the confidence that the track is acoustic

Danceability - describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity

Energy - measure from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity; typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy

Instrumentalness - predicts whether a track contains no vocals; the closer the instrumentalness value is to 1.0, the greater likelihood the track contains no vocal content

Liveness - detects the presence of an audience in the recording; higher liveness values represent an increased probability that the track was performed live

Speechiness - detects the presence of spoken words in a track; the more exclusively speech-like the recording, the closer to 1.0 the attribute value

Valence - measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed; tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry)

The entire setlist consists of tracks with zero instrumentalness. Only a few tracks have high acousticness - The A Team, Give Ne Love, Visiting Hours, and Afterglow. One track has a reasonably high liveness value, Don't; You Need Me, I Don't Need You has a medium liveness value. Both songs consist of rap verses with a hook. The majority of songs on the setlist have high danceability, energy, and valence values. These make sense for songs being performed at a concert meant to entertain people.
