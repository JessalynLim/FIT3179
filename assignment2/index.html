Background Information

The +–=÷× Tour (pronounced The Mathematics Tour) is the ongoing fourth concert tour by English singer-songwriter Ed Sheeran. Comprising of 89 concerts across three legs, the tour commenced on 23 April 2022 in Dublin, Ireland, and was originally scheduled to conclude on 23 September 2023 in Inglewood, California. A rescheduled concert will be held on 28 October 2023 as a replacement for the cancelled 9 September 2023 concert in Paradise, Nevada. Thus, this concert is not included.

The tour is in support of his fifth studio album = (pronounced Equals), released 2021, and his sixth studio album − (pronounced Subtract), released 2023. Due to – being released after the Europe leg, songs from – were only occassionally performed. Songs from – were also performed on 24 June 2023, when Ed Sheeran performed as both opening and main act. Additionally, some songs from his seventh studio album Autumn Variations, released 2023, were performed at his final show in Inglewood, California.



Revenue Around the World

The Mathematic Tour is considered a world tour, however, Ed Sheeran only performed in Europe, Oceania, and North America. Not one concert was performed in Africa nor Asia.

Ed Sheeran performed multiple concerts at several locations, including major cities such as London (England), Cardiff (Wales), Frankfurt (Germany), Brisbane (Australia), Aucklund (New Zealand), and East Rutherford (United States).

The size of the circles indicate the revenue per concert. London had the highest revenue, followed by Brisbane. Thus, both also had the greatest revenue per concert for their respective legs of the tour (Europe and Oceania).The concert in East Rutherland, the closest to New York City, had the highest revenue for Leg 3 of the tour (North America). However, this amount of revenue is similar to the majority of per concert revenue in Europe.



Overall Attendance

These donut charts show the attendance compared to the possible capacity for all three legs: Europe, Oceania, and North America. It is clearly evident that both Oceania and North America had 100% attendance, completely using all capacity available. The Europe leg, the first, was still very well attended, with 98% attendance, but it is clear that there was extra capacity that wasn't filled by fans.




Revenue, Capacity, and Attendance

Over Time

The charts above show the revenue, attendance, and capacity over the course of the tour, as well as the cumulative values. We can see that there is a sharp peak in all three around July 2022, which can be attributed to the 5 shows at Wembley Stadium, London. There is a second peak in March-April 2023, which can be attributed to the shows held in Australia. The attendance and capacity chart on the top right concurs the story illustrated by the donut charts above; attendance is only less than capacity for the Europe leg of the tour.

From the linecharts that show cumulative variables, we can observe trends in the gradient, i.e. the rate of change in revenue, attendance, and capacity. The first two legs, Europe and Oceania, appear to have similar rates of increase. In contrast, the third leg, North America, has a more gradual rate of increase. The charts above also show the dates of the three legs and the gaps between; leg 1 (Europe), from May to September 2022, leg 2 (Oceania), from February to March 2023, and leg 3 (North America), from May to September 2023.

Revenue Against Attendance

As expected, there is a positive, linear relationship between attendance and revenue. Using the slider to highlight the month the concerts were held, we can observed that there are no concerts from October to January.

The only concerts held in February to March were part of the Oceania leg of the tour. Additionally, all of the Oceania leg was held in March and February. The only concerts held in April were part of the Europe leg.

This chart also clearly shows an outlier for all five shows at Wembley Stadium in London, England. They have the greatest revenue, nearly $40,000, and the greatest attendence, over 400,000. The concert with the next greatest revenue and attendance has just over half the revenue and attendance.
